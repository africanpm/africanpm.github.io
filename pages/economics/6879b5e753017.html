    The impact of modern economic exploitation can be viewed from the perspective of the USA, China and Russia to better understand the devastating effect of bad economic policy that is crippling Africa:

  • China

    Economic Influence: China is Africa's largest bilateral lender, holding significant amounts of African debt.Infrastructure Development: China's investments in infrastructure and resource extraction have led to accusations of debt-trap diplomacy, where countries become overly reliant on Chinese loans and investments.

    Resource Extraction: China is a major buyer of African minerals and metals, raising concerns about the equitable distribution of benefits and the environmental impact of resource extraction.

    Labor Practices: Some Chinese-run mining companies have faced criticism for exploitative labor practices, including low wages and poor working conditions.

  • RUSSIA

    Military and Security Support: Russia provides military support and security assistance to several African nations, often with the goal of bolstering its influence and countering Western interests.

    Resource Extraction: Wagner Group, a Russian private military company, has been involved in securing mining concessions, particularly for gold, in exchange for military support.

    Exploitation of Instability: Russia is accused of exploiting instability and security vacuums in West Africa to expand its influence.

  • USA

    Economic Interests: The US has economic interests in Africa, particularly in access to natural resources and trade partnerships.

    Promoting Democracy: The US has advocated for democratic governance and human rights in Africa, which can sometimes conflict with its broader geopolitical objectives.

    Historical Legacy: The US also has a historical legacy of engagement with Africa, including the slave trade, which continues to be a source of tension and criticism.
