WOMAN PARTICIPATION IN AFRICAN POLITICS:

Women’s empowerment advocates in Africa are making their voices heard, with the full backing of international organisations such as the United Nations and the African Union. In the fight for gender equality, women around the world have advanced; yet its progress seems to be foot-dragging for African women. Significant progress can be seen in the appointment of women in key ministerial positions. There are 30% more women ministers of defence, 52.9% more women ministers of finance, and 13.6% more women ministers of foreign affairs. This is a sharp contrast to the “soft issue” portfolios, such as social affairs, children and family. In sub-Saharan Africa, the number of women seated in parliament grew in 2018, with a regional average share of 23.7%, according to the 2019 edition of the biennial “Inter-Parliamentary Union (IPU) Map of Women in Politics”.

Hence, Africa needs to maintain this momentum, more women in politics lead to more inclusive decisions, Phumzile Mlambo-Ngcuka UN Women Executive Director and former Deputy President of South Africa, states: “We still have a steep road ahead, but the growing proportion of women ministers is encouraging, especially where we see a rise in the number of countries with gender-balanced ministerial cabinets”. Globally and especially Moslem-controlled states still have discriminatory laws that limit women’s ability to run for political offices. As countries strive to implement Sustainable Development Goal 5, “Achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls,” governments must also strive to embed gender parity in constitutions and legal frameworks; eliminate all forms of violence against women and ensure that women, like men, receive a quality education.

WOMEN VIZ A VIZ POLITICS:

Africa has made a significant stride towards allowing women to participate actively in politics, unlike the Middle East where women are generally repressed. Women’s equal participation and leadership in political and public life are essential to achieving the Sustainable Development Goals by 2030. However, data show that women are under-represented at all levels of decision-making worldwide and that achieving gender parity in political life is far off.

As of 19 September 2022, there are 28 countries where 30 women serve as Heads of State and/or Government. At the current rate, gender equality in the highest positions of power will not be reached for another 130 years. Just 13 countries have a woman Head of State, and 15 countries have a woman Head of Government. Only 21 per cent of government ministers were women, with only 14 countries having achieved 50 per cent or more women in cabinets. With an annual increase of just 0.52 percentage points, gender parity in ministerial positions will not be achieved before 2077.

Globally, there are 24 States in which women account for less than 10 per cent of parliamentarians in single or lower houses, including three single/lower chambers with no women at all.
