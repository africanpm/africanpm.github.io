Political Perspective of Over-reliance on Genetically Modified Crops

Agriculture remains the potent medium to achieving food security. And governance simply defined, primes itself with protection of life and properties of which food is an essential ingredient keeping us alive. One needs to be alive to enjoy dividends of good governance; thus, governments across the globe prioritise agriculture and subsidizing farmers to ensure steady supply of food. Advancement in technology made large scale farming possible through the invention of farming machineries and storage facilities. With these, plus proper irrigation channels; farming all year round is now visible.

With every innovation comes a down side, mechanised farming often leaves the soil dilapidated, thereby making crops yield less subsequent planting seasons. This low yielding problem of a result excessive farming, coupled with crop-bore-diseases prompted the development of GMOs. Genetically modified crops yields better and are resist to common pest/disease that hampers growth and bumper harvest. Unfortunately, technology comes at a cost and the country or company with the patent dictates the tone of its usage and set the profit margins in this current capitalist driven economy.

How does Africa fair in GMOs Politics?

Africa is at a cross road best described with the analogy of between the devil and the deep blue sea. The deep blue sea in this contest profiles Africa as a desperate nation, in dare need of food security for her teaming population; hence, they need GMOs that is capable of high yields to achieve food sustainability, which if managed well with the existing technology (Mechanised farming and Storage facilitates) can lead to food surplus for the continent. Thereby providing the needed food security at the shortest possible time; but at the detriment of her local crops/market.

On the other hand, the devil in this contest can be viewed as the capitalist who deciphered a means to sterilize these crops/seedlings (GMOs), thereby becoming the sole distributor of the fertile variants. This implies that the food security of any nation that uses these steril GMOs are in the hands of the company or country with the patent for the fertile variant of these genetically modified seeds.
